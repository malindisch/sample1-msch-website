critical care services

casualty Accident & Emergency

Our Casualty Accident & Emergency (A&E) Centre provides services 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. The A & E department entrance is strategically located imediately on the left side of the Hospital main entrance and is easily accessible by ambulances and private vehicles.


At Malindi Sub-County Hospital, we understand that accidents and emergencies can happen at any time, and our dedicated Accident and Emergency (A&E) department is here to provide you with immediate and expert medical care when you need it most. Our highly trained medical professionals work tirelessly to ensure the best possible outcomes for all our patients.
Per Article 43 of the Constitution of Kenya, we provide emergency medical treatment to all patients. You do not require a referral to access emergency services.

Here are some key features of our Accident and Emergency services:

»This is an entry point for all admission cases

»Our main goal is to evaluate the patient, conduct the required emergency intervention in consultation with the relevant o n-call specialists, and stabilise patients before moving to other departments.

»24/7 Emergency Care: We operate around the clock, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Whether it's the middle of the night or a public holiday, our A&E department is always ready to respond to emergencies promptly.
»Rapid Response Team: Our skilled team of emergency physicians, nurses, and support staff are equipped to handle a wide range of medical emergencies, from minor injuries to critical conditions. We prioritize quick assessment, diagnosis, and treatment to ensure the best chances of recovery.

»State-of-the-Art Facilities: Our hospital is equipped with advanced medical technology and equipment to support efficient diagnosis and treatment. We maintain a well-equipped resuscitation area and trauma bays, enabling us to provide comprehensive care in critical situations.

»Multidisciplinary Approach: Our A&E department follows a multidisciplinary approach to emergency care. Our medical team collaborates with specialists from various departments within the hospital to ensure seamless coordination and comprehensive treatment for our patients.

»Triage System: To prioritize patient care based on the severity of their condition, we employ a triage system. This system allows us to identify and provide immediate care to those in critical condition while ensuring that patients with less urgent conditions receive appropriate attention in a timely manner.


»Services at the department are supported by a 24-hr A&E laboratory service, A&E Pharmacy, and an A&E X-ray Unit.

high dependency unit

The High Dependency Unit (HDU) and Newborn Unit (NBU) at Malindi Sub-County Hospital provide specialized care for critically ill patients and newborns, respectively.Both units are staffed by the best team.Experienced to provide the highest level of care.
The Critical Care Department is supported by a team of experienced Medical Specialists, Medical Officers and Critical Care Nurses

Both the HDU and NICU at Malindi Sub-County Hospital are accredited by the Kenya Ministry of Health. This means that they meet the highest standards of care. The hospital also has a strong commitment to research and education. The staff at the HDU and NICU are involved in a number of research projects that are aimed at improving the care of critically ill and premature babies


ambulance services

Ambulance Services and crew are an essential part of the Accident and Emergency department. These services include:


1.Basic Life Support Services (BCLS): Entails transfer of patients who do not need critical care.

2.Advanced Critical Life Support service (ACLS): Entails moving patients who need critical care services


If you're a new here, there's no doubt that your top priority is ensuring you health and well-being and that of your loved ones. At Malindi Sub-County hospital, we understand this better than anyone else - which is why we offer high quality services at our facility.
