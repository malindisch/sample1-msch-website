our comprehensive care clinic services (ccc)

Our clinics offer holistic and easily accessible HIV and TB care and treatment services which include:

  • HIV Counselling and Testing.
  • HIV Prevention Services – Post Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP), Pre-exposure Prophylaxis (PrEP).
  • Prevention of Mother-to-Child Transmission (PMTCT).
  • HIV/AIDS Care and Treatment – Adult and Pediatric.
  • TB Care and Treatment.
  • TB Screening and prevention services.
  • Laboratory support – CD4, Viral load.
  • Nutritionist Review.
  • Counseling

MSCH CCC aims to provide high-quality, sustainable, HIV care and treatment services that are readily available and accessible to its clients.

We also work to reduce AIDS-associated mortality by expanding the capacity of MSCH to manage the advanced and complex presentation of HIV infection, including second-line and third-line ART failure, HIV /MDR and XDR TB co-infection, complicated drug-drug interactions, and severe adverse drug interactions in PLHIV and severe systemic infectious and non-communicable co-morbidities.

To achieve this goal, CCC services are available not only in the outpatient but also in the inpatient and transit patients. This allows for continuity of care for the clients. Transit patients are those seeking other services within the facility e.g, cancer care, but on follow-up for HIV care elsewhere.

here are some brief details about TB care and treatment services offered at Malindi Sub-County Hospital:

1.Screening and diagnosis: The hospital has a dedicated TB clinic that offers free screening and diagnosis services. Patients can be tested for TB through a sputum sample or a chest X-ray.

2.Treatment: The hospital provides free TB treatment to all patients. Treatment is a long-term process that usually lasts for 6-9 months. Patients are given a combination of antibiotics that they must take every day.

3.Follow-up care: Patients are required to attend regular follow-up appointments at the hospital to ensure that they are taking their medication correctly and that the TB is being cured.

4.Prevention and education: The hospital also offers TB prevention and education services. These services include providing information about TB, how to prevent it, and how to get tested and treated.

The hospital boasts of a multi-disciplinary approach to patient care. HIV/AIDS care services are integrated with the rest of the hospital. Clients can access different specialist reviews available through the intra-departmental referral system.

If you are concerned that you or someone you know may have TB, please contact Malindi Sub-County Hospital for more information. The hospital's TB clinic is open Monday-Friday from 8am to 4pm. You can call the clinic at +254 722 222 222 or visit the hospital's website at www.malindihospital.org.

If you're a new here, there's no doubt that your top priority is ensuring you health and well-being and that of your loved ones. At Malindi Sub-County hospital, we understand this better than anyone else - which is why we offer high quality services at our facility.
